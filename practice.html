Geology and Earth Science Study Guide

A comprehensive overview of key concepts in geology and Earth science.

Volcanoes

  • Mt. Washburn is a stratovolcano that post-dates the last caldera-forming eruption at Yellowstone.
  • Volcanic arcs are associated with subduction zones.
  • Shield volcanoes are mostly constructed of basaltic flows.
  • Andesite is an igneous volcanic rock.
  • Which type of volcano is made of interbedded lava flows and pyroclastic deposits? Stratovolcano

Igneous Rocks

  • What is formed when magma crystallizes slowly underground? Coarse-grained, intrusive igneous rock.
  • The grain size of an igneous rock imparts important information about cooling rate.
  • Mafic silicate minerals containing iron tend to weather primarily by oxidation.
  • Peridotite has the least amount of silica content among rock types.
  • Fractional crystallization involves the separation of crystals and melt during cooling.

Sedimentary Rocks

  • Coarser-grained sediments are usually closer to their original sources and deposited in higher-energy environments.
  • Which type of sediment settles from water flowing at the highest velocity? Gravel.
  • The mineral or material most resistant to weathering is quartz.
  • Stream beds upstream of dams experience more silting, while stream beds downstream experience more erosion.

Metamorphic Rocks

  • Which type of rock is formed by recrystallization at elevated temperature and pressure? Metamorphic.
  • The protolith of schist is shale.

Earth's Structure

  • Volumetrically, the Earth is mostly silicate rock.
  • The Earth's inner core is composed of solid iron-nickel, while the outer core is liquid.
  • Slow convection of solid rock occurs in the Earth's deep mantle.
  • What is the composition of the Earth's inner core? Solid iron-nickel.

Plate Tectonics

  • Regions of the seafloor with negative magnetic anomalies were formed during times when the Earth's magnetic field had reversed polarity.
  • The last major supercontinent was named Pangea and broke apart approximately 200 million years ago.
  • When two segments of oceanic lithosphere converge, the older segment will get subducted.
  • The San Andreas Fault is located along a transform plate boundary.
  • The Pacific Plate is moving to the northwest, as evidenced by the Hawaiian Island chain.

Coastal Erosion

  • According to Orrin Pilkey, seawalls cause erosion and steepening of the shoreface in front of the wall.
  • The best long-term response to coastal erosion along developed shorelines is the relocation of buildings.
  • Barrier islands are moving towards the mainland, exposing older mud deposits on their seaward side.

Dating Methods

  • Using radioactive isotopes to date rocks is the basis of absolute dating.
  • Index fossils are found over a wide area and existed during a relatively short period of geologic time.
  • Which of the following materials could be dated with the Carbon-14 dating technique? A piece of wood ~2000 years old.
