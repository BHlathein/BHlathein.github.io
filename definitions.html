Geology Definitions

Geology and Core Concepts

Geology

The scientific study of Earth's physical structure, processes, and history.

Physical Geology

Focuses on Earth's materials (minerals, rocks) and processes (volcanism, erosion).

Science

The systematic study of the natural world through observation and experimentation.

Uniformitarianism

The principle that the processes operating today also occurred in the past at similar rates.

Hypothesis

A testable explanation for observations or phenomena.

Theory

A well-substantiated explanation of natural phenomena supported by evidence.

Minerals

Mineral

A naturally occurring, inorganic solid with a definite chemical composition and crystal structure.

Crystal

A solid material whose atoms are arranged in a repeating pattern.

Polymorphs

Minerals with the same chemical composition but different crystal structures, e.g., diamond and graphite.

Crystallization

The process of forming crystals from magma or solutions.

Precipitation

The formation of a solid mineral from a solution.

Vein

A mineral-filled crack in rock.

Solid-State Diffusion

The movement of atoms through a solid to form new minerals.

Polymerization

The linking of molecules into a structured network, common in silicate minerals.

Common Minerals

  • Quartz: A hard, silicate mineral resistant to weathering.
  • Plagioclase Feldspar: A group of feldspar minerals rich in sodium and calcium.
  • Potassium Feldspar: A feldspar mineral rich in potassium.
  • Muscovite Mica: A light-colored silicate mineral that splits into thin sheets.
  • Olivine: A green, iron-magnesium silicate mineral.
  • Pyroxene: A group of dark, single-chain silicate minerals.
  • Amphibole: Dark-colored silicates with double-chain structures.
  • Biotite Mica: A dark-colored mica mineral that splits into sheets.

Rock Types

  • Rock: A naturally occurring solid composed of one or more minerals.
  • Igneous: Formed from cooled magma or lava.
  • Sedimentary: Formed from lithified sediments.
  • Metamorphic: Formed by heat and pressure altering pre-existing rocks.

Intrusive / Plutonic

Igneous rocks that cool slowly beneath the Earth's surface, forming large crystals.

Extrusive / Volcanic

Igneous rocks that cool quickly at the Earth's surface, forming fine grains.

Volcanoes and Igneous Structures

  • Shield Volcano: Broad, gently sloping volcanoes formed by fluid lava flows.
  • Cinder Cone: Small, steep-sided volcanoes formed from pyroclastic fragments.
  • Stratovolcano / Composite Cone: Large, steep volcanoes built from alternating lava flows and pyroclastics.
  • Caldera: A large depression formed when a volcano collapses after an eruption.
  • Pahoehoe: Smooth, ropy lava flows.
  • Aa: Jagged, rough lava flows.

Sedimentary Features

  • Mudstone / Shale: Fine-grained sedimentary rocks.
  • Sandstone: Lithified sand-sized grains.
  • Conglomerate: Sedimentary rock made of rounded pebbles and cobbles.
  • Cross-Bedding: Inclined sedimentary layers indicating wind or water currents.
  • Mud Cracks: Features formed when wet sediment dries and contracts.

Metamorphic Processes

  • Protolith: The original rock before metamorphism.
  • Foliation: The alignment of minerals under directed pressure.
  • Garnet: A common metamorphic mineral.
  • Slate: A fine-grained foliated metamorphic rock.
  • Marble: Metamorphosed limestone composed of calcite.
